# District-8-Vacant-Lands-Dashboard
City Hall Project

This project was meant to study the potential developmental land in District 8 of San Jose. As a district with the highest percentage of vacant land, conducting an extensive analysis was important. 

To carry out this project, I utilized public data from the City, County, and the San Francisco Chronicle's Property Map. The .ipynb file attached to this repository follows the process I took to organize the data. 

Then, I put the data into Tableau to better communicate and visualize findings for non-technical audiences. 

The [Vacant Plots Map](https://public.tableau.com/shared/B52HXBKNN?:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link) includes all of the parcels designated “vacant” by the City of San Jose, and thus noted in the GIS shapefile published online and updated daily. Additional data collection went into finding (a) potential property owners' names, (2) zoning designations, and (3) additional useful data (such as density). 

District 8 has a total of 285.3 acres of vacant land. More specifically, when adding parcels with acreage greater than one, District 8 has a total of 275.6 acres. 

San Jose has envisioned building around 170,000 units by 2040. 

Impact: This dashboard helped the office understand key spots for development and the zoning makeup for these potential spaces. Additionally, this map allows staff to visualize who owns various vacant parcels of land for future purposes.
